What should you not say on a dating site?



When it comes to online dating, it’s important to be aware of what you should and should not say on a dating site. Here are five things you should never put on a dating profile:

1. What You’re Not Looking For: Including criteria for who shouldn’t swipe left or message you is just a real Debbie Downer move. It’s best to keep the focus on the positive and what you are looking for in a potential match.

2. Anything Too Generic: It’s important to be specific and make your profile stand out from the crowd. Generic phrases like “I like to have fun” or “I’m looking for someone special” won’t help you make a connection.

3. Sarcasm About Being on ‘The Apps’: It’s understandable to be a bit frustrated with the online dating process, but sarcasm isn’t the way to go. Instead, focus on the positive aspects of online dating and be open to meeting new people.

4. Lies (or Even Exaggerations): It’s important to be honest on your dating profile. Don’t lie about your age, job, or interests. Even exaggerating the truth can lead to problems down the line.

5. No Information: Don’t leave your profile blank or without any information. It’s important to include details about yourself, such as your interests, hobbies, and goals. This will help potential matches get to know you better.

By avoiding these five things, you can ensure that your dating profile is successful and you’ll be able to make meaningful connections with potential matches. Good luck!
