5 Signs to Spot a Dating Scammer



Online dating has become increasingly popular over the years, and with it, the risk of being scammed. Dating scammers are individuals who create fake profiles on dating sites with the intention of deceiving other users and taking advantage of them financially. Here are five tell-tale signs to help you spot a romance scam.

1. The Scammer is Quick to Call it Love

One of the most common signs of a dating scammer is when they profess their love for you very quickly. Scammers often use emotional language to try and make you feel guilty or ashamed if you don't reciprocate their feelings. If someone is claiming to love you after only a few conversations, it is likely a scam.

2. The Scammer's Profile Contains Vague or Few Images Across Platforms

Another sign of a dating scammer is if their profile contains few or vague images across different platforms. Scammers often use stock photos or images taken from other people's profiles to create their own. If the profile images look too good to be true, it is likely a scam.

3. The Scammer Asks for Money

Another sign of a dating scammer is if they ask for money. Scammers often use stories of financial hardship or illness to try and guilt you into sending them money. If someone you are talking to asks for money, it is likely a scam.

4. Allow Relationships to Develop Slowly and Naturally

It is important to take your time when getting to know someone online. Don't rush into a relationship or give out too much personal information. Allow relationships to develop slowly and naturally, and be wary of anyone who is trying to rush you into a commitment.

5. Set Up a Video Chat to Verify Their Identity

Finally, it is important to set up a video chat to verify the identity of the person you are talking to. Scammers often use fake names and photos, so it is important to make sure the person you are talking to is who they say they are.

By being aware of the signs of a dating scammer, you can protect yourself from becoming a victim. If you think you may be talking to a scammer, it is important to take steps to verify their identity and to end the conversation.
