What is hardballing in dating?

Hardballing is a dating term that refers to a clear expression of relationship expectations. It was coined by Logan Ury, the director of relationship science at Hinge. In this article, we will explore what hardballing is and its impact on dating.

Definition of Hardballing

Hardballing is the act of being upfront about one's relationship goals and expectations. It can refer to either a desire for a serious long-term partnership or a casual fling. The key feature of hardballing is its direct and intentional nature. This contrasts with the more traditional approach of waiting to see how a relationship develops and discussing these topics at a later stage.

Advantages of Hardballing

One of the main advantages of hardballing is that it saves time. By being clear about your relationship goals from the start, you can quickly determine whether the person you are dating is on the same page. This can prevent you from investing time and energy in a relationship that is not going to meet your needs.

Another advantage of hardballing is that it eliminates misunderstandings. When you are clear about your expectations, there is less room for misinterpretation. This can help to avoid frustration and disappointment down the line.

Impact on Dating

Hardballing can have both positive and negative impacts on dating. On the one hand, it can help to create more efficient and fulfilling relationships. By being clear about your goals and expectations, you can build a relationship that is tailored to your needs and desires.

On the other hand, hardballing can be perceived as being too direct or confrontational. Some people may feel that it takes away from the romance and excitement of dating. It is important to consider the individual you are dating and whether hardballing is an appropriate approach for them.

In conclusion, hardballing is a new dating term that refers to being clear about one's relationship expectations. It can save time and eliminate misunderstandings but can also be perceived as too direct. Whether hardballing is the right approach for you will depend on your individual preferences and the person you are dating.
