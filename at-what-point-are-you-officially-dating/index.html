At what point are you officially dating?

Dating is a subjective experience that varies from person to person and relationship to relationship. While some couples may know they are officially dating after just a few dates, others may not know until much later on. So, at what point are you officially dating someone?


According to a relationship expert, it is socially acceptable to broach the subject of dating after two months of spending time together. This is because two months provides enough time for both individuals to get to know each other and determine if they have a good fit. However, this timeline may not apply to everyone, as some individuals may reach the official dating stage earlier or later than two months.


The key factor in determining whether you are officially dating someone is the amount of time you spend together. If you are spending a significant amount of time with someone and having a positive experience, you may consider yourself to be officially dating. This can include spending time together on a regular basis, going on dates, and engaging in meaningful conversations.


Another important factor is the level of comfort you feel around the other person. If you are comfortable around someone and feel at ease in their presence, this can be a good indicator that you are officially dating. Additionally, if you are comfortable introducing them to your friends, this can be a sign that you are taking the relationship to the next level.


In conclusion, the point at which you are officially dating someone depends on various factors such as the amount of time you spend together, the level of comfort you feel around each other, and your overall experience. While two months may be a socially acceptable time to broach the subject of dating, it ultimately depends on the individual relationship and the individuals involved. If you are unsure whether you are officially dating someone, try introducing them to your friends and see how they react.
