What age is late for marriage?


Marriage is an important decision that affects the rest of your life. Knowing when the right time to marry is a question that has been asked for centuries. In recent years, research has been conducted to determine the ideal age for marriage.

What Does the Research Say?

Research conducted by the University of Utah in 2017 sought to answer the question of what age is late for marriage. The study looked at over 1,000 people who married between the ages of 18 and 32. For men, early was defined as marrying before the age of 26 (29 percent), on time was between the ages of 27 and 30 (38 percent), and late if they married after 30 (33 percent). Results showed that people who married on time or late were least likely to report depressive symptoms in midlife.

Benefits of Marrying On Time or Late

The research found that people who married on time or late had fewer depressive symptoms in midlife. This could be due to the fact that people who marry later in life are more likely to have achieved their educational and career goals, which can lead to greater life satisfaction. Additionally, people who marry later may have had more time to develop emotionally and become more mature.

Challenges of Marrying Late

One of the challenges of marrying late is that it can be more difficult to conceive a child. Women over the age of 35 have a higher risk of infertility and pregnancy complications. Additionally, people who marry late may have less time to enjoy their marriage due to the increased risk of age-related health issues.

Conclusion

The research conducted by the University of Utah found that people who married on time or late were least likely to report depressive symptoms in midlife. However, it is important to consider the potential challenges of marrying late, such as increased risk of infertility and age-related health issues. Ultimately, the decision of when to marry should be based on personal preferences and goals.
