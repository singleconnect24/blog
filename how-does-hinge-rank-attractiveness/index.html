How Does Hinge Rank Attractiveness?


Hinge is a popular dating app that uses an algorithm to match users with people who are likely to mutually like one another. It measures this based off of user engagement and preferences. This article will discuss how Hinge ranks attractiveness and how it works.

What is the Gale-Shapley Algorithm?

Hinge uses the Gale-Shapley algorithm to rank attractiveness. This algorithm is a matching algorithm that was developed in the 1960s and is used to match two sets of people. It is based on the idea that two people can mutually benefit from being paired together. The algorithm works by having each person rank the other person in order of preference. It then pairs the two people who are most likely to mutually like one another.

How Does Hinge Rank Attractiveness?

Hinge uses the Gale-Shapley algorithm to rank attractiveness. It does this by measuring user engagement and preferences. It looks at how often a user interacts with other users, how often they are matched with people who have similar preferences, and how often they are liked by other users. The more engagement a user has, the higher their attractiveness ranking will be.

What Other Factors Does Hinge Consider?

In addition to user engagement and preferences, Hinge also takes into account a user's profile information. This includes things like age, location, interests, and other personal information. This helps Hinge determine which users are more likely to be compatible with each other.
