Why do they call Virginia the state for lovers?


Virginia is known as the state for lovers due to its romantic scenery, history, and attractions. The slogan was first used in 1969 by a copywriter from the Richmond advertising agency Martin & Woltz. The slogan was intended to capture the romantic nature of the state and to attract tourists.

History of the Slogan

In 1969, a $100-a-week copywriter named Robin McLaughlin in the Richmond advertising agency Martin & Woltz came up with the slogan “Virginia is for history lovers” that could be adapted for beach lovers, mountain lovers and so on. This slogan was intended to capture the romantic nature of the state and to attract tourists. The slogan was first used in a television commercial and quickly became popular. It has since been used in various forms and has become a part of the state’s identity.
