What should you not say on a dating site?


Dating sites are an excellent way to meet potential partners, but it is important to know what to avoid when creating a profile. Here are five things you should never put on a dating profile.

What You're Not Looking For

When creating a dating profile, it is important to be honest about what you are looking for. However, it is not necessary to include criteria for who should not message you or swipe left. This can come off as a Debbie Downer move and may turn potential matches away.

Anything Too Generic

When creating a dating profile, it is important to make sure that it is unique and stands out from the rest. Avoid using generic phrases or clich√©s that could be used on any profile. Instead, try to be creative and make your profile as interesting as possible.

Sarcasm About Being on 'The Apps'

While it may be tempting to make jokes about being on a dating site, it is important to remember that not everyone will find it funny. This type of sarcasm can come off as negative and can turn potential matches away.

Lies (or Even Exaggerations)

When creating a dating profile, it is important to be honest. Do not lie or exaggerate about yourself or your interests. Not only is this dishonest, but it can also be dangerous.

No Information

It is important to include some information about yourself on your dating profile. Do not leave your profile blank, as this will not give potential matches enough information to decide if they are interested in you.

The Dos and Don'ts of Dating Site Profiles

Creating a dating profile can be intimidating, but it is important to remember that honesty is the best policy. Avoid including criteria for who should not message you, using generic phrases, making jokes about being on the apps, lying or exaggerating, and leaving your profile blank. Following these tips will help you create a successful dating profile that will attract potential matches.
