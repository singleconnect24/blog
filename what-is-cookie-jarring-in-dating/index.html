What is Cookie Jarring in Dating?


Cookie-jarring is a dating phenomenon that is becoming increasingly common. It is when someone leads another person to believe that they have a connection that will lead to a relationship, but in reality, they have no intention of ever committing. This can be emotionally damaging for the person who is being misled and can lead to feelings of betrayal and hurt.

Definition of Cookie Jarring

Cookie-jarring is a form of emotional manipulation in which one person leads another to believe that a romantic relationship is possible, when in reality, they have no intention of ever committing. This is done by stringing the other person along, making them believe that there is a chance of a relationship developing, while knowing that it will not.

How Does Cookie Jarring Work?

Cookie-jarring works by making the other person feel special and wanted, while not actually committing to them. This can be done by giving compliments, making plans, and flirting, but never actually taking the relationship to the next level. This can leave the other person feeling confused and betrayed, as they were led to believe that there was a chance of a relationship, when in reality, there was not.

Signs of Cookie Jarring

It can be difficult to tell if someone is cookie-jarring you, as they may be good at hiding their true intentions. However, there are some signs that can indicate that you are being cookie-jarred. These include:

• Making plans but never following through

• Not introducing you to their friends or family

• Constantly changing the subject when the topic of relationships comes up

• Not making any effort to take the relationship to the next level

• Making excuses for why they can’t commit

• Flirting with other people

• Not being available when you need them

• Not being honest about their feelings

How to Avoid Being Cookie Jarred

The best way to avoid being cookie-jarred is to be clear about your expectations and boundaries from the start. Make sure that you are both on the same page about what you want from the relationship, and don’t be afraid to ask questions if you are unsure. Additionally, it is important to trust your instincts and be aware of any red flags that may indicate that the other person is not being honest with you.

Cookie-jarring is a form of emotional manipulation that can be damaging to those who are on the receiving end. It is important to be aware of the signs of cookie-jarring and to trust your instincts when it comes to relationships. Additionally, it is important to be clear about your expectations and boundaries from the start, so that you can avoid being misled.
