What is the rule of 7 for dating?

Dating can be a tricky business, especially when it comes to navigating the social norms surrounding age differences in relationships. The "half-your-age-plus-seven" rule is a commonly cited guideline to determine whether an age difference is considered socially acceptable.

Half-your-age-plus-seven rule

The "half-your-age-plus-seven" rule states that a person should never date someone whose age is less than half their own age plus seven years. This rule is often used as a general reference to gauge whether a particular age gap is considered socially acceptable.

Age disparity in sexual relationships

The topic of age disparity in relationships has been studied and discussed for many years, with researchers and psychologists exploring the implications and effects of age differences on relationships. The general consensus is that relationships with larger age gaps tend to be more challenging and may come with unique social and cultural challenges.

Common dating mistakes

-Asking the wrong questions on a first date

-Selling oneself too aggressively

-Being too focused on sex

-Not being a gentleman/gentlewoman

-Not following up or indicating interest after the date
