Why would a scammer ask for a selfie?


In today’s digital age, it’s not uncommon for scammers to ask for a selfie. Fraudsters are using this tactic to gain access to personal information, steal identities, and launder money. In this article, we’ll discuss why scammers are asking for selfies and what you can do to protect yourself.

Why Fraudsters Want Selfies with ID Cards

One of the most common reasons why scammers are asking for selfies is to gain access to personal information. Some online services require a photo with ID for registration. If you send a selfie to scammers, they will be able to create accounts in your name — for example, on cryptocurrency exchanges — with a view to using them to launder money.

In addition, scammers may use the selfie to create a false identity. They can use the photo to create a fake profile on social media or other websites, or even use it to apply for credit cards or loans in your name.

How to Protect Yourself

It’s important to be aware of the risks associated with sending selfies to scammers. Here are some tips to help you protect yourself:

• Never send a selfie to anyone you don’t know.

• Don’t provide any personal information, such as your address or phone number, to anyone you don’t know.

• Don’t respond to any requests for money or personal information.

• Be wary of any emails or messages that ask for a selfie.

• Don’t click on any links in suspicious emails or messages.

• Be careful when using public Wi-Fi networks.

• Make sure your computer and mobile devices are protected with antivirus software.
