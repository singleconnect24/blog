What is a woman's big three?

A natal chart is a representation of the alignment of celestial bodies at the time, date, and location of a person's birth. It provides insight into an individual's personality traits, characteristics, and life path.

The "big three" in a natal chart refers to the sun, moon, and rising (or ascendant) placements. These placements provide valuable information about an individual and play a significant role in shaping their personality and life path.

Sun Placement

The sun placement in a natal chart represents the individual's core identity and the qualities they strive to embody. It reveals the person's strengths, passions, and purpose in life.

Moon Placement

The moon placement in a natal chart represents the individual's emotional and instinctual self. It reveals their emotional needs, habits, and how they handle relationships.

Rising (Ascendant) Placement

The rising (ascendant) placement in a natal chart represents the individual's outer personality and how they present themselves to the world. It reveals their physical appearance, mannerisms, and social behavior.

The sun, moon, and rising placements in a natal chart, also known as the "big three," provide valuable insight into an individual's core identity, emotional self, and outer personality. Understanding these placements can help individuals better understand themselves and their place in the world. It's important to remember that a natal chart is just one tool for self-discovery and should not be used to define or limit an individual's potential.
