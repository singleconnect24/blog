Cheating Most Likely to Occur on Fridays at 6.45pm


Cheating is a common occurrence in relationships, and it can be hard to pinpoint when it is most likely to happen. A survey of 1,000 members from extramarital dating site, IllicitEncounters, has revealed that the day cheating is most likely to occur is Friday, and more specifically at 6.45pm.

Day and Time of Most Common Affairs

The survey revealed that cheating tends to most commonly occur twice a week, with the second most popular time being Tuesday nights. Friday evenings at 6.45pm were found to be the most common time for an affair to take place.

Reasons for Friday Cheating

There are a few possible reasons why Friday evenings are the most popular time for cheating. For many people, the weekend is a time to relax and let loose, and this can lead to making bad decisions. Additionally, the weekend can be a time for couples to spend time apart, which can make it easier for one partner to sneak away for an affair.

Other Popular Times for Cheating

The survey also revealed that cheating is most likely to occur in the evening, with 8pm being the second most popular time for an affair. This is likely due to the fact that most people are home from work and have more free time in the evening.

Tips to Avoid Cheating

If you are worried about your partner cheating, there are a few things you can do to reduce the risk. Make sure to spend quality time together, especially on the weekends. Talk openly about your relationship and any issues you may be having. Finally, be aware of any changes in your partner's behavior that could indicate they are cheating.
