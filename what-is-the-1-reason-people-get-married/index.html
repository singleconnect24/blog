What is the #1 reason people get married?

Getting married is a big decision, and it's one that many people take seriously. But what is the number one reason people decide to get married? While there are many reasons why people choose to tie the knot, love is often cited as the primary motivation. In this article, we'll explore why love is the number one reason people get married and how it affects their decision-making process.

What is the #1 Reason People Get Married?

Among married and cohabiting adults, love is cited more than any other reason for why they decided to get married or to move in with their partner: 90% of those who are married and 73% of those living with a partner say love was a major factor in their decision. This is according to a survey conducted by the National Center for Health Statistics in Nov 6, 2019.

Love is a powerful emotion, and it's often the primary motivation for people to get married. For many, the desire to share their life with someone they love is a strong enough reason to take the plunge and get married. Love can also be a powerful motivator to make the commitment to stay together and work through any issues that may arise.

Other Reasons People Get Married

While love is the number one reason people get married, there are other factors that come into play. For some, the desire to start a family or to have a legal and social recognition of their relationship may be a factor. Financial security and stability may also be a motivating factor for some couples.
