Which state is best for a single person?

What is the best state for a single person? This is a question that many people ask when they are looking for a place to live that is ideal for their lifestyle. With so many states to choose from, it can be difficult to determine which one is the best fit. To help make the decision easier, WalletHub recently released a study that ranks the best and worst states for singles.

Which State is Best for a Single Person?

When it comes to finding the best state for a single person, there are a few factors to consider. These include the cost of living, the availability of jobs, the quality of life, and the number of singles in the area. WalletHub took all of these factors into account when creating their list of the best and worst states for singles.

Best States for Singles

WalletHub's study found that the best states for singles are Florida, Texas, Pennsylvania, Illinois, Massachusetts, New Jersey, Wisconsin, and Virginia. Each of these states scored highly in terms of affordability, job opportunities, quality of life, and the number of singles in the area.

Florida scored the highest overall, with an impressive score of 71.6 out of 100. This was followed by Texas with a score of 69.5, Pennsylvania with a score of 68.9, and Illinois with a score of 68.2. Massachusetts, New Jersey, Wisconsin, and Virginia rounded out the top eight with scores ranging from 66.3 to 65.3.

Worst States for Singles

On the other end of the spectrum, the worst states for singles are Alaska, West Virginia, Mississippi, Arkansas, Louisiana, South Dakota, North Dakota, and Wyoming. These states scored poorly in terms of affordability, job opportunities, quality of life, and the number of singles in the area.

Alaska scored the lowest overall, with a score of 43.2 out of 100. This was followed by West Virginia with a score of 44.7, Mississippi with a score of 45.3, and Arkansas with a score of 45.7. Louisiana, South Dakota, North Dakota, and Wyoming rounded out the bottom eight with scores ranging from 46.3 to 47.3.

Conclusion

When it comes to finding the best state for a single person, WalletHub's study provides a helpful guide. Florida, Texas, Pennsylvania, Illinois, Massachusetts, New Jersey, Wisconsin, and Virginia are the best states for singles, while Alaska, West Virginia, Mississippi, Arkansas, Louisiana, South Dakota, North Dakota, and Wyoming are the worst. No matter which state you choose, make sure to do your research and find the one that best suits your needs.
